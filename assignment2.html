Extra Implementations

1. Method 1 — Center-Evaluated Smooth Terms

Instead of evaluating everything at the random light sample x'_k, this method evaluates the smooth terms at the center of the stratum c_k.

For each stratum:

c_k  = deterministic center of the cell
x'_k = random point inside that same cell

Then compute:

f(x, wi(c_k), wo)
G(x, c_k)
V(x, x'_k)

Estimator:

Ld ≈ Li * (A / N) * Σ f(x, wi(c_k), wo) * G(x, c_k) * V(x, x'_k)

This means the BRDF and geometry terms are deterministic, while visibility remains random. This reduces noise because only the shadow test is random.

Visual Result

Fully lit areas become smoother because they are no longer affected by random BRDF or distance variation. Noise mostly remains near shadow boundaries, where some samples are visible and some are blocked.

Tradeoff

This method is slightly biased because it does not evaluate the full integrand at the exact same random sample location. It mixes a center-sampled smooth term with a randomly sampled visibility term.

2. Method 2 — Analytic Formula Per Stratum

Method 2 improves on Method 1 by computing the exact analytic unshadowed irradiance for each stratum, then multiplying that stratum's analytic contribution by one sampled visibility value.

Per-stratum contribution:

contribution_k ≈ analytic_unshadowed_contribution_for_stratum_k * V(x, x'_k)

Each stratum is treated as a mini area light. If the whole light is visible, the result becomes exactly the analytic solution with zero noise. If part of the light is blocked, noise only appears near soft-shadow boundaries because visibility is the only random part.

Why It Is Better Than Method 1

Method 1 evaluates the smooth terms at the stratum center, which is only an approximation. Method 2 integrates the smooth unoccluded irradiance over the whole stratum analytically, so the non-visibility part is much more accurate.

Implementation Notes

Method 1

Method 1 changes only the Monte Carlo area-light estimator.

Current behavior:

BRDF, geometry, and visibility are all evaluated at random sample x'_k.

Method 1 behavior:

BRDF and geometry are evaluated at stratum center c_k.
Visibility is evaluated at random sample x'_k.

With lightstratify on, each stratum has stable BRDF and geometry terms evaluated at the cell center, while visibility is randomly evaluated inside the cell. Smooth lighting variation becomes much less noisy, and noise mostly remains near shadow boundaries.

Important Behavior

This method only meaningfully activates when:

  • integrator direct is enabled
  • lightstratify on is enabled
  • lightsamples is a perfect square

If lightstratify is off, the code falls back to the original random Monte Carlo behavior because there are no stratum centers to use.

Method 2

Method 2 makes each stratum act like a tiny parallelogram light. Instead of evaluating the smooth terms at the stratum center, it analytically integrates the unshadowed diffuse contribution over the entire stratum, then multiplies by one sampled visibility value.

For each stratum:

  1. Build a smaller QuadLight representing that stratum.
  2. Pick one random point inside the stratum.
  3. Trace one visibility ray to that random point.
  4. If visible, add the analytic diffuse contribution from the whole stratum. If blocked, add nothing.

Method 1 structure:

BRDF/G at stratum center
visibility at random point

Method 2 structure:

diffuse irradiance analytically over the whole stratum
visibility at random point

This is why fully visible regions become nearly noise-free.

Analytic formula:

Ld = (kd / pi) * Li * irradiance

For Method 2, the implementation is:

Diffuse: analytic per stratum
Specular: keep Method 1 center-sampled approximation
Visibility: random sample inside stratum
